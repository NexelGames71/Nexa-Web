    3.1. Explanatory Man: Ever find yourself staring blankly at your screen, paralyzed by choices? That's decision fatigue—a psychological phenomenon where making too many decisions wears down your mental stamina, like running sprints until your legs give out.
  
    3.2. Explanatory Man: The average professional makes over 35,000 decisions a day, and every unread email is another micro-choice. Each time you decide whether to reply, archive, or ignore, your focus chips away.
  
    3.3. Explanatory Man: Now, you might ask, 'Isn't it just a matter of willpower?' Actually, it's more like battery power. Once depleted, even the smallest task can feel overwhelming.
  
    3.4. Explanatory Man: To combat this, leverage 'batch processing'—grouping similar tasks together, just like doing laundry instead of washing every sock individually. And embrace 'email scheduling,' which is like setting office hours for your attention.
  
    3.5. Explanatory Man: By understanding decision fatigue, you can structure your workflow to protect your most valuable resource: your brainpower.
